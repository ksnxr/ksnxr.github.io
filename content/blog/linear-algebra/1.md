## Lecture 1

### problem

$2x-y=0,\quad -x+2y=3.$

$A=\begin{bmatrix}2&-1\\-1&2\end{bmatrix},\quad X=\begin{bmatrix}x\\y\end{bmatrix},\quad b=\begin{bmatrix}0\\3\end{bmatrix}.$

Solve $AX=b$.

### row picture

$b$ is the sum of the rows of $A$ times $X$.

### column picture

$x\begin{bmatrix}2\\-1\end{bmatrix}+y\begin{bmatrix}-1\\2\end{bmatrix}=b$

$b$ is linear combination of the columns of $A$.

Linear combinations of the columns of $A$ fill the whole plane.

### matrix picture

$A$ is called the *coefficient matrix*, $x$ is the *vector of unknowns*.

### linear independence

Given a matrix $A$, if we can not solve $Ax=b$ for every possible vector $b$, then $A$ is a *singular matrix*, and its column vectors are *linearly dependent*.
